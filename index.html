01 · WACV 2026
Enhancing Vision Language Corruption Robustness using Cross-Distribution & Prompted Denoisers
Our work studies the robustness of Vision-Language Models (VLMs) under realistic multimodal corruptions. While modern VLMs perform impressively under ideal conditions, their performance drops significantly when faced with real-world noise such as blurry images or grammatically incorrect text. To investigate this problem, we introduce VLSRB, a new multimodal corruption and denoising benchmark containing 18 visual and 18 textual corruption functions designed to evaluate the system robustness of VLMs. To improve robustness, we propose: • Cross-distribution visual denoisers inspired by the Mixture-of-Experts framework • A prompted zero-shot textual denoiser
